# 🎯 Business Problem

## Background

A national retail company ("Superstore") sells furniture, office supplies, and
technology products to consumers, corporate clients, and home offices across the
United States. Leadership has plenty of sales data but limited visibility into
**where profit is actually coming from** — and where it is quietly leaking away.

Sales are growing, but profit is not growing at the same pace. Management
suspects that heavy discounting and certain low-margin products may be hurting
the bottom line, but they need data to confirm it.

## The Core Question

> **"We are selling a lot — but are we selling profitably? And what should we do
> about it?"**

## Analytical Questions This Project Answers

1. **Sales & Profit Overview**
   - What are total sales and total profit for the period?
   - What is the overall profit margin?

2. **Regional Performance**
   - Which regions generate the most sales and profit?
   - Are any regions selling a lot but earning little profit?

3. **Product Performance**
   - Which product categories and sub-categories are most and least profitable?
   - Are any products losing money?

4. **Customer Segments**
   - Which customer segment (Consumer, Corporate, Home Office) is most valuable?

5. **Discount Impact**
   - How do discounts affect profit?
   - At what discount level does profit turn negative?

6. **Trends Over Time**
   - How have sales and profit changed year over year?

## Who Would Use This

- **Sales leadership** — to focus on profitable regions and products
- **Pricing/merchandising teams** — to fix discount and product-mix problems
- **Executives** — to see the health of the business at a glance

## Success Criteria

The project is successful if it produces:
- A clear picture of profit drivers and profit drains
- An interactive dashboard that non-technical stakeholders can read in minutes
- A short list of concrete, data-backed recommendations
