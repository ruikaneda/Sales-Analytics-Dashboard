# ✅ Recommendations

Data-backed actions based on the insights in `business_insights.md`. Each
recommendation names the problem, the action, and the expected impact — the way
a real analyst presents to stakeholders.

> 📝 Recommendations reference example figures. Confirm the underlying numbers
> from your own analysis before presenting.

---

## 1. Cap discounts to stop selling at a loss

- **Problem:** Average profit turns negative once discounts pass ~20–30%.
- **Action:** Set a **maximum standard discount (e.g., 20%)** and require
  manager approval above it. Review any product routinely sold above that level.
- **Expected impact:** Directly recovers profit currently lost on deep-discount
  orders — likely the highest-ROI change available.
- **Owner:** Pricing / Sales Operations

---

## 2. Fix or phase out money-losing products

- **Problem:** Tables and Bookcases lose money overall.
- **Action:** For each losing sub-category, choose one path: **(a)** renegotiate
  supplier cost, **(b)** raise price / cut discount, or **(c)** discontinue the
  worst individual SKUs.
- **Expected impact:** Removes a persistent drain on profit without touching
  healthy product lines.
- **Owner:** Merchandising / Category Management

---

## 3. Double down on Technology and hero products

- **Problem:** Profit is concentrated in a few categories and products, but
  they may be under-promoted.
- **Action:** Prioritize **Technology** (especially Copiers, Phones,
  Accessories) and the **top-10 profit products** in marketing, homepage
  placement, and inventory planning.
- **Expected impact:** Grows the most profitable revenue rather than just total
  revenue.
- **Owner:** Marketing / Merchandising

---

## 4. Investigate the Central region's weak margin

- **Problem:** Central sells reasonably but converts less of it into profit.
- **Action:** Compare Central's **discount levels and product mix** against
  West/East to find the root cause, then apply the same discount and mix
  discipline.
- **Expected impact:** Lifts a lagging region toward company-average
  profitability.
- **Owner:** Regional Sales Leadership

---

## 5. Grow Corporate and Home Office segments

- **Problem:** Consumer dominates; other segments may be underdeveloped.
- **Action:** Test **targeted offers and account outreach** for Corporate and
  Home Office buyers, who often place larger, more predictable orders.
- **Expected impact:** Diversifies revenue and reduces reliance on a single
  segment.
- **Owner:** Sales / Account Management

---

## 6. Build a recurring profitability dashboard

- **Problem:** These issues were invisible until someone looked.
- **Action:** Publish this Tableau dashboard as a **monthly standard report** so
  leadership tracks profit — not just sales — continuously.
- **Expected impact:** Turns a one-time analysis into an ongoing early-warning
  system.
- **Owner:** Analytics / BI Team

---

## 🎯 Priority Order (if you can only do a few)

1. **Cap discounts** (fastest, biggest profit recovery)
2. **Fix money-losing products**
3. **Investigate Central region**
4. Promote Technology, grow segments, and stand up the dashboard
